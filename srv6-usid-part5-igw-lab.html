What is the SRv6 IGW?

You already know both halves of this lab

Parts 1–4 gave you the SRv6 side: IS-IS, locators, End.DT4 SIDs, VPNv4 over IPv6 BGP sessions. The MPLS side (OSPF + SR-MPLS + VPNv4 over IPv4 BGP) is the pre-SRv6 world that already exists in most SP networks — nothing conceptually new. The IGW is the only genuinely new node. Everything else you have already configured in some form. Treat Part 5 as two familiar halves finally meeting at a boundary, not a new mountain to climb.

In a pure SRv6 network every PE uses an End.DT4 SID as the VPN service identifier. But real networks migrate in phases. An existing MPLS domain uses VPN labels (allocated by BGP labeled-unicast or SR-MPLS). The two domains cannot exchange VPN services directly because they use incompatible transport identifiers.

The SRv6 Interworking Gateway (IGW) sits at the domain boundary and solves this:

  • Toward the SRv6 domain: IGW allocates an End.DT4 SID for each VRF, advertised via BGP VPNv4 to SRv6 PEs. SRv6 PEs send VPN traffic as outer IPv6 packets addressed to that SID.
  • Toward the MPLS domain: IGW allocates an MPLS VPN label for each VRF, advertised via BGP VPNv4 to MPLS PEs. MPLS PEs send VPN traffic as labelled packets.
  • At the data plane: IGW decapsulates whichever encapsulation arrived and re-encapsulates in the other format before forwarding.

The CE routers and MPLS PEs have no knowledge of SRv6. The SRv6 PEs have no knowledge of MPLS labels. The IGW is the only node that speaks both.

IGW vs non-speaker CE

A CE that does not run BGP (Part 4 right-side static CEs) is a “non-speaker” at the CE-PE boundary — it just does not participate in the PE-CE routing protocol. That is a PE-CE concern only. The IGW is a completely different concept: it operates at the PE-to-PE transport boundary between two network domains. The IGW needs no CE at all to demonstrate its function.

Step 13 — IGW BGP: Dual-Transport VPNv4

IGW maintains two types of VPNv4 iBGP sessions simultaneously:

  • IPv6 sessions to PE5–PE8 (fc00:0:5–8::1) — SRv6 transport
  • IPv4 session to MPE1 (10.200.0.2) — MPLS transport
Why retain route-target all on the IGW?

By default, a BGP speaker only keeps VPNv4 routes whose Route Target (RT) matches a locally configured VRF. The IGW sits at the domain boundary and must re-advertise routes between the two domains: routes arriving from MPE1 (RT 65000:100) must be forwarded to PE5–PE8, and routes arriving from the SRv6 PEs must be forwarded to MPE1. Without retain route-target all, the IGW would silently discard any route whose RT does not match a locally imported VRF — breaking the interworking even though all BGP sessions are up. This command is only needed on nodes that act as domain boundary translators; regular PE5–PE8 do not need it.

✓ Pass: 10/10 — IPv4 VPN traffic traverses SRv6 domain, IGW translation, and MPLS domain end-to-end

15.2 — What the IGW actually did

The path for a single ICMP packet:

  1. PE5 encapsulates IPv4 ICMP in SRv6 outer header — dst = FCBB:BB00:9:E000:: (IGW End.DT4 for CUST-A)
  2. P1 → P3 forward the outer IPv6 packet using IS-IS routes; no awareness of VPN
  3. IGW receives the SRv6 packet, matches End.DT4 SID, strips the outer IPv6 header, looks up inner IPv4 (10.200.100.1) in VRF CUST-A, and pushes the MPLS VPN label for MPE1
  4. MP1 swaps/forwards the MPLS label stack toward MPE1
  5. MPE1 pops the VPN label, looks up 10.200.100.1 in its VRF CUST-A, and delivers to Loopback100

15.3 — Reverse path: MPLS PE to SRv6 PE

RP/0/RP0/CPU0:MPE1# ping vrf CUST-A 192.168.50.1 source 10.200.100.1 repeat 10

✓ Pass: 10/10 — symmetric path, IGW translates MPLS→SRv6 on the return

Troubleshooting

IGW BGP session to MPE1 stays Active

  • IGW uses Loopback1 (10.200.0.9) as update-source for the IPv4 session. Confirm OSPF has a route to 10.200.0.2 on the IGW: show route ipv4 10.200.0.2/32
  • MP1 must forward packets between IGW and MPE1 — check show mpls forwarding on MP1 for labels 16009 and 16012.

End.DT4 SID not appearing on IGW

  • Confirm segment-routing srv6 locator MAIN alloc mode per-vrf is present under vrf CUST-A address-family ipv4 unicast in the BGP config.
  • The SID is allocated only after the VRF BGP AF is active — check show bgp vrf CUST-A ipv4 unicast first.

MPLS VPN label not appearing on IGW

  • Confirm segment-routing mpls alloc mode per-vrf is also present under the same VRF BGP AF.
  • This dual allocation (SRv6 SID + MPLS label) is the defining feature of the IGW. If only SRv6 is configured, MPE1 will not receive a usable label.

PE5 CEF shows IGW End.DT4 SID but ping fails

  • Check that IGW can reach MPE1’s VRF prefix: show route vrf CUST-A on IGW must include 10.200.100.0/24.
  • Check the MPLS path: ping 10.200.100.1 source 10.200.0.9 from the global table on IGW (non-VRF) to confirm the MPLS path is up before testing the VRF path.

IS-IS shows IGW as neighbor on P3 but SRv6 PEs can’t reach fc00:0:9::1

  • Verify P3’s IS-IS config includes interface GigabitEthernet0/0/0/5 with point-to-point and address-family ipv6 unicast.
  • The IS-IS NET on IGW must be unique: 49.0001.0000.0000.0009.00.
